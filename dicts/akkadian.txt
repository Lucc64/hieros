Prepositions

Akkadian has prepositions which consist mainly of only one word. For example: 
ina (in, on, out, through, under), 
ana (an, too, for, after, approximately), 
adi (to, da:zu), 
aššu (cause, because of), 
eli (up, over, lift <> alive?), 
ištu/ultu (of, *ist, since, ultima “last”?), 
mala (in accordance with < malike?), 
itti (*vitt: with, and also <> like?). 
-im ~ mi,mit,im,am,und

There are, however, some compound prepositions which are combined with ina and ana (e.g. 
ina maḫar (forwards), 
ina balu (without), 
ana ṣēr (up to), 
ana maḫar (forwards)

. Regardless of the complexity of the preposition, the following noun is always in the genitive case.

Examples: 
ina bītim ('in Bett' : in the house, from the house)
ana dummuqim (to do good) ?
itti šarrim (with the king), ana ṣēr mārīšu (up to his son).

The Akkadian vocabulary is mostly of Semitic origin. 
Although classified as 'East Semitic', many elements of its basic vocabulary find no parallels in related Semitic languages!!!

For example:

qātu 'hand' (Semitic *yd), <> khand khapta!

qabû 'say' (Semitic *qwl),  <> qiz @ Goth
izuzzu 'stand' (Semitic *qwm),  <> ??
ana 'to, for' (Semitic *li). <> AN !

Due to extensive contact with Sumerian and Aramaic, the Akkadian vocabulary contains many loan words from these languages.

Aramaic loan words, however, were limited to the 1st centuries of the 1st millennium BC and primarily in the north and middle parts of Mesopotamia, whereas Sumerian loan words were spread in the whole linguistic area. Beside the previous languages, some nouns were borrowed from Hurrian(𒄷𒌨𒊑), Kassite, Ugaritic and other ancient languages.
